# Resident Rights

The civic charter. Short, explicit, public. Printed on the first screen of onboarding, in every buurthuis, and on the Stoep homepage.

These are commitments, not aspirations. Each one has technical and [[Governance|governance]] consequences.

## The charter

### 1. You own what you write

Your posts, comments, photos, and contributions are yours. You retain copyright. You can edit or delete them at any time. You can export everything you've ever contributed in a standard format (ActivityPub, plus a plain markdown archive).

### 2. You can leave cleanly

If you leave Stoep, you can take your content with you, ask that all copies be deleted, or both. Federated copies propagate deletion. No "deleted account" residue sitting in a database for five years.

### 3. No ads, ever

Not banner ads. Not sponsored posts. Not "promoted by a local business." Not discovery slots paid for by the gemeente. Not affiliate links. Never. This is in the coöperatie's statuten and cannot be changed without dissolving and reforming under a different name.

### 4. No algorithmic ranking

Your feed is chronological and proximity-scoped. No engagement ranking, no "For You," no "based on your activity." The order you see is the order things happened.

### 5. Your data stays in the EU

Servers in the Netherlands or EU-hosted infrastructure only. No transfer of personal data outside the EU, ever. This is both AVG compliance and political commitment.

### 6. No third-party tracking

No Google Analytics. No Meta pixels. No Segment, no PostHog-cloud, no Amplitude. If usage measurement is needed, it is self-hosted, aggregated, and the methodology is public.

### 7. Your data is not sold, shared, or brokered

Not to advertisers, not to data brokers, not to insurance companies, not to the gemeente. Ever. Aggregated, anonymised buurt-level statistics may be published as open data — with the methodology, the anonymisation thresholds, and the opt-out all public. Individual data stays with you.

### 8. No dark patterns

No growth hacks that manipulate your attention. No notification manipulation. No "are you sure you want to leave" guilt screens. No default-on tracking settings. If Stoep wants your attention, it has to earn it honestly.

### 9. Your identity is yours

Your verified buurtpaspoort is portable across federated instances. You can change which buurt you're verified in when you move. You can dispute verification errors. You can request verification via alternative paths if BAG doesn't work for you — see [[Identity-edge-cases]].

### 10. Moderation is visible

Moderator actions on your content are visible to you, with reasoning. The buurt's norms are publicly written and publicly amended. You can appeal any moderation decision to the buurt council and, beyond that, to the ethics committee. See [[Governance]].

### 11. Gemeente has no special view of you

The gemeente does not see private messages. Does not see who voted for what in a buurtbudget consultation (aggregates only). Does not get resident lists. Does not have backend access. Gemeente sees what any resident sees, plus the civic data gemeente itself provides.

### 12. You are not data

You are a resident. Your worth on this platform is not measured in posts, followers, likes, reactions, streaks, or badges. None of those exist. There is no status hierarchy built into the product.

## Commitments with teeth

These rights are weak if they're only words. Mechanisms:

- **Statuten.** The charter is written into the coöperatie's founding documents. Changing it requires two-thirds of the ledenvergadering and a public consultation.
- **Canary.** Annual transparency report lists any request for data from gemeente, law enforcement, or any third party, with response. If no requests received, the report says so. Absence of the canary is a signal.
- **Audit.** Independent AVG audit annually. Findings published in full.
- **DPIA.** Done before launch, republished whenever the data model changes materially. Open document.
- **Code is open.** Every line is AGPL. Residents (or their representatives) can verify claims by reading the source.

## What the charter is not

Not a terms-of-service. Not a legal contract that Stoep can update unilaterally at midnight. Not marketing copy. This is a constitution for the platform, structurally harder to change than a feature.

## Residents' bill of obligations (minimal)

Rights come with a small shared responsibility:

- Be truthful about who you are (no fake identities, no impersonation).
- Respect buurt norms (publicly written, locally evolved).
- No harassment, hate speech, or targeted attacks.
- No commercial misuse of resident identity.
- No campaign activity during protected election windows. See [[Governance]].

Violations are handled by [[Governance|moderation-as-civic-role]], not by dark ToS-triggered bans.
