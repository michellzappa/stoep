# Smaller internets

Stoep does not try to fix the internet. It tries to build a **miniverse** — digital infrastructure scoped to one community's needs, on a different set of principles than the platforms everyone already uses.

That posture has a name. In late 2025, [co—matter mapped an emerging ecosystem](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/nothing-personal/the-post-naive-internet-era/) (Mozilla Foundation, *Nothing Personal*) as the **post-naive internet**: builders who traded grand idealism ("a free and open internet for everyone") for realism ("infrastructure primarily for *our* community, on *our* terms"). The map is useful vocabulary for how Stoep should be pitched — and what it should refuse to become.

## From naive to post-naive

The naive era assumed scale and universality were the path to change. The post-naive era assumes **conditions** matter more than reach: who owns the platform, what values are baked in, how the feed works, who governs.

| Naive posture | Post-naive posture |
|---|---|
| Replace big tech for everyone | Build for a specific community's needs |
| Metaverse-scale environments | **Miniverses** — islands, not seas |
| Growth as proof of impact | Legitimacy, sustainability, cultural conditions |
| "The internet" as one thing to fix | Many smaller internets, interconnected |

Stoep is unambiguously post-naive: one buurt (or federated cluster of buurten), not a Dutch Nextdoor. Success is not MAU; it is whether parochial trust and civic legibility improve in participating buurten. See [[Precedents-and-evidence]].

## Four layers (and where Stoep sits)

co—matter argues post-naive projects differ on four layers. Stoep should be legible on all four — not just technically.

### 1. Structural

Ownership and economics shape everything downstream. Examples in the map: [Subvert](https://subvert.fm/) (cooperatively owned music platform after the Bandcamp sale), platform co-ops generally.

**Stoep:** [[Governance|Coöperatie U.A.]] stewardship, no equity exit, gemeente as commissioner not owner. AGPL commons layer. This is the Subvert lesson applied to buurt life: structurally different *before* the community scales, not retrofitted after a VC round.

### 2. Value

What the platform optimises for. Example: [Metalabel](https://metalabel.com/) — collaboration and revenue splits over competition.

**Stoep:** civic function fused with social texture — meldingen, buurtbudget, inspraak — not engagement or ad inventory. See [[Concepts|civic interface layer]] and [[Open-data]].

### 3. Design

Interface choices encode ideology. Example: *Perfectly Imperfect* — human recommendations over algorithmic feeds.

**Stoep:** [[Concepts|no engagement metrics]], proximity-scoped chronological feed, multilingual-native, [[Concepts|buurt-digest]] as the weekly reason to return. The product should feel *smaller than one WhatsApp group* at default scope (see wireframe copy on `/feed`).

### 4. Community

Governance close to the people who use it. Example: [Trust](https://trust.support/) (Berlin) — IRL hub plus experiments in decentralised governance.

**Stoep:** [[Concepts|moderation-as-civic-role]], buurt councils, [[Concepts|offline bridges]], physical launch via buurtconnectors. Digital without the buurthuis noticeboard is incomplete.

## Miniverse, not metaverse

A miniverse serves a bounded community with bounded norms. It does not ask residents to relocate their whole digital life.

That maps cleanly onto Stoep's scope model:

- **Default:** your straat and adjacent blocks (~200 neighbors), not Amsterdam.
- **Federation upward** only when the topic warrants it (OV, city-wide consultation) — see [[Concepts|federation]] and [[Vision]].
- **Not a replacement for WhatsApp.** Intimate, urgent chatter stays where it works. Stoep is the persistent civic layer underneath.

The Mozilla piece's insight: these islands are not lonely. Media, hubs, catalysts, and platforms form an **ecosystem** — Trust, Serpentine Arts Technologies, Restless Egg, Bluesky's AT Protocol, Nina, fediverse instances, zines, schools. Stoep should plug into that ecosystem early ([[Risks-and-next-steps|Waag, PublicSpaces, SocialHub]]) rather than narrate itself as a standalone app.

## What this changes in the pitch

1. **Stop apologising for small scope.** Post-naive builders accept the internet of today and carve islands. A buurt pilot is the point, not a stepping stone to "the Dutch Facebook."
2. **Lead with structure, not features.** Funders and gemeentes have seen a hundred neighborhood apps. Few have seen one designed as Ostrom-style commons from day one. See [[Governance]] and [[Precedents-and-evidence|Ostrom]].
3. **Culture-war framing, carefully.** The article argues culture is now fought over *infrastructure* — who owns distribution, who sets norms. Stoep is infrastructure for parochial trust and civic participation, not content moderation at global scale.
4. **Interconnection is credibility.** Stoep is stronger positioned as one node in the post-naive / smaller-internet ecosystem (fediverse, PublicSpaces, Dutch digital sovereignty) than as a clever local startup.

## Risks of misreading the frame

- **Post-naive is not cynical.** It is pragmatic. Stoep still bets that digital infrastructure can make values real — just for a buurt first.
- **Small is not an excuse for extractive design.** Burner-phone dystopia and platform extractivism are the backdrop; Stoep must be structurally unlike Nextdoor, not merely smaller.
- **Federation is not automatic virtue.** Blockchains did not democratise; ActivityPub only helps if governance and adoption match. See [[Technical-stack|federation politics]].
